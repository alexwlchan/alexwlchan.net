Clicking on one of the characters, I learnt that these are from the [Palmyrene alphabet][wiki-palymyrene-alphabet].

One of the cool things about Unicode

https://www.unicode.org/L2/L2010/10003-n3749-palmyrene.pdf

> The Palmyrene alphabet was used from the first century BCE, in a small independent state established near the Red Sea, north of the Syrian desert between Damascus and the Euphrates. The alphabet was derived as a national script by modification of the customary forms that cursive Aramaic which themselves developed during the first Persian Empire. Palmyrene is known from documents distributed over a period from the year 9 BCE until 273 CE, the date of the sack of Palmyra by Aurelian.

Not only have I never heard of Palmyrene, I haven't even heard of the character names used.

These are some of the symbols shown:

* Letters *pe* (𐡱), *heth* (𐡧), *zayin* (𐡦)
* Letter *nun* (𐡮), which also has a final form (𐡭) – this reminds me of *sigma* in Greek, which looks different depending on whether it's in the middle or the end of a word (σ/ς)
