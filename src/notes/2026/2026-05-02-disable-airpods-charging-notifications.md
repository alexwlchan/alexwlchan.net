Every morning, I wake up to a notification on my iPhone:

> Alex's AirPods Pro 2 are fully charged.

This is not a surprise.
This is not news.
This is something iOS could have worked out will always be the case, because I have a charging stand where I put my AirPods overnight.
My AirPods are *always* fully charged when I wake up.

I have, on multiple occasions, searched the "Notifications" area of the iPhone Settings app to find where I can disable this notification, to no avail.

Today, I got a respite: I found an [Apple Support document][apple-support] that explains where to configure charging notifications:

> You can use your iPhone or iPad to […] notify you when charging is complete.
>
> 1. Put your AirPods in your ears, and make sure they’re connected to your iPhone or iPad.
> 2. On your iPhone or iPad, go to Settings, select your AirPods, then tap Battery.
> 3. Turn on Charging Notifications.

I have no idea why these aren't also available in the Notifications settings, but I can *finally* stop these pointless messages.

[apple-support]: https://support.apple.com/en-gb/119912
